2025 Technion Mechanical Engineering Graduate Research Day

Gaze and Motion Synergies in VR Reaching Tasks towards Multi- Joint Prosthetic Arm Control

E. Gerber, N. Krausz

This study investigates gaze and motion synergies during reaching tasks to improve the control of multi-joint prosthetic arms. By analyzing kinematic data in egocentric coordinates, we found that gaze velocity typically anticipates hand velocity and that their positions converge prior to a grasp. Current efforts involve developing a multi-modal platform to record sEMG, gaze, and kinematic data during real-world activities to leverage early motor synergies for user intent prediction.

2025 Karniel Computational Motor Control Workshop

Preliminary Results of Gaze Tracking and Hand Motor Synergy Coordination During Reach-to-Grasp VR Tasks for Improved Prosthetic Arm Intent Prediction

E. Gerber, N. Krausz

This research investigates the spatiotemporal coordination between gaze and hand motion to address the high cognitive burden of current sequential prosthetic control. By transforming VR reaching data into a specialized "eye-hand" reference frame, we identified that gaze peak acceleration consistently leads hand peak acceleration by approximately 150 ms. While this temporal synergy suggests that user intent can be inferred early in the motion, we are moving toward a multi-modal approach—integrating sEMG, kinematics, and gaze—to develop a more robust, naturalistic prosthesis controller.
