About me

The person behind the research

I am an MSc student in the Faculty of Mechanical Engineering at the Technion — Israel Institute of Technology, where I work on understanding and predicting human upper-limb motion using multimodal sensing. My research lies at the intersection of biomedical engineering, machine learning, and human-robot interaction.

My current project focuses on forecasting 3D hand and arm kinematics in real time by fusing high-frequency surface EMG with gaze data and positional markers. I build hybrid deep-learning architectures (CNN + GRU) designed to run causally, making them suitable for deployment in assistive robotics and prosthetics.

Outside the lab, I enjoy photography — particularly wildlife — and I believe that careful observation, whether through a camera lens or a sensor array, is the foundation of good science.
